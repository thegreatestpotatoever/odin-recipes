Lasagne

a slice of delicious lasagne

Steps

  1. On a clean surface, place approx 2 cups of flour and a generous pinch of salt in a small mound and make a well in the middle.
  2. In the flour well place 3 whole eggs and 2 egg yolks, using a form mix the eggs and then gradually incorporate the flour into the egg mix using the fork at first and then your clean hands.
  3. Knead the dough for about 2-5 mins until it forms a smooth dough.
  4. Cover the dough with a clean, damp tea towel and let it rest for approx 5-10mins.
  5. In a large heavy bottomed pot heated to medium-high temp, add approx 1/4 cup olive oil and finely chopped onions and celery cooking until onions become translucent.
  6. Add finely minced garlic to pot and cook for approx 2mins, stirring occasionally.
  7. Add 2tbsp of Dried Chilli Flakes, 1tbsp of Freshly Ground Pepper, and 1 tbsp of Dried Rosemary and stir into mixture letting cook for about 1-2mins or until fragrant.
  8. Add 3tbsp of Tomato Paste to pot and stir cooking until it becomes a deep red colour.
  9. Add 500g Pork Mince and 500g Beef Mince to pot, cooking until the colour changes.
  10. Pour in 1 cup of Red Wine to pot, deglasing anything stuck to the bottom of the pot with a wooden spoon.
  11. Pour in 750ml of Tomato Passata into pot and mix thoroughly, then add 4 Bay Leaves to the mixture.
  12. Bring mixture in pot to a boil, then reduce heat to a gentle simmer with the lid on but not quite covering the pot entirely.
  13. Preheat oven to 180 degrees celsius, convert this to farenheit or kelvin if you must.
  14. Using a rolling pin, or a pasta roller if you have one, roll out the pasta dough on a clean floured surface to approx 3mm thickness and sufficient surface area for 2-3 complete sheets that will fit in dish you are using for your lasagne.
  15. In a new saucepan, add 100g of Butter and melt using a medium-high temp.
  16. Add 1tbsp of Freshly Ground Pepper, and 1tsp of grated Nutmeg to butter in pan.
  17. Add 1/2 cup of flour to melted butter and stir to incorporate.
  18. Pouring in approx 1 cup of milk at a time and stirring to incorporate with melted butter and flour mixture, only adding the next cup of milk when the mixture is homogenous, until you have incorporated at least 1 ltr of milk making adjustments to suit your desired viscosity.
  19. Set the Bechmal sauce aside off heat with a lid resting on top.
  20. In a food processor add 1/2 cup of Pine Nuts, a large bunch of Basil leaves and stems, and 1/2 cup of Olive Oil and blend until smooth.
  21. In the Lasagne dish of choice, place enough of the Bolognese sauce to cover the bottom of the dish.
  22. Place the first sheet of Lasagne on top of the Bolognese sauce in the dish.
  23. Spoon about 1/2 to 1/3 of the pesto on top of the Lasagne sheet, adjusting depending on how many layers of Lasagne you are making.
  24. Place an even layer of Bechmal sauce into the Lasagne dish approx 1 1/3 cups of Bechmal depending on how much you love Bechmal.
  25. Grate Parmesan Reggiano over the Bechmal sauce in the Lasagne dish.
  26. Repeat the last 5 steps until you've run out of Lasagne sheets.
  27. Place the Lasagne dish into the pre-heated oven for approx 30-45mins or until the top of the Lasagne is sufficiently cooked for your personal preference.
  28. Remove the Lasagne from the oven and allow to rest for 15-20mins before serving.
